We Found $96,000/Year in Wasted Time at One Company

Here's a real example. A 15-person company. No unusual inefficiencies. Just normal business operations.

We mapped every recurring task across every department. Added up the hours. Did the math at average cost per hour.

40 hours per week of tasks that AI could handle. At $50/hour average loaded cost: $96,000 per year.

Not theory. Not projection. Actual tasks, actual hours, confirmed by the people doing them.

The tasks weren't exotic. Report compilation. Data entry from one system to another. First-draft emails. Meeting summaries. Status updates. Research that follows the same pattern every time. Onboarding checklists.

None of these tasks require human judgment. They require human time. That's the difference AI closes.

Today's Exercise • 30 minutes

Step 1: Send this table to every department head. Ask them to fill it out for their top 10 recurring tasks:
Task Who Hrs/wk Needs judgment? AI could handle?
Weekly status report Sarah 3 No Yes
Client follow-up emails Mike 5 Some Partially
... ... ... ... ...

Step 2: Calculate your numbers.

  • Sum the "Hours/week" column for tasks where AI could handle = fully or partially
  • Multiply by your average loaded hourly cost (salary + benefits + overhead, divided by hours)
  • Multiply by 48 (working weeks per year)

Step 3: Identify your top 3. The three tasks with the highest hours that AI could handle. These are your first AI agents. These are where you start.
