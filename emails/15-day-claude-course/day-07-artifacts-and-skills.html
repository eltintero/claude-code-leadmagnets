Claude Just Built a Spreadsheet, a Slide Deck, and a PDF From My Notes

Up until now, you've been having conversations with Claude. Today, Claude starts building things.

Artifacts are interactive, reusable outputs that Claude creates alongside its response. Not just text — actual working documents:

  • Interactive charts and dashboards
  • Formatted documents and reports
  • Spreadsheets with formulas
  • Presentation slides
  • Code that you can run and test
  • SVG diagrams and flowcharts

You can iterate on them ("make the chart bigger," "add a column for Q3"), download them, and share them. They're real deliverables, not just chat responses.

Skills are expertise packages that teach Claude specialized tasks. Think of them as reusable instruction sets. Claude has built-in Skills (writing, analysis, coding), and you can create custom ones.

Remember your Brand Voice Guide from Day 2? Today you'll turn it into a Skill that any team member can activate with one click.

Today's Exercise • 25 minutes

Step 1: Create a Brand Voice Skill.

Go to your Claude Profile → Skills → Create Skill. Name it "Write in [Company Name] Voice." Paste your brand-voice.md content as the instruction. Now test it: start a new conversation, activate the Skill, and ask Claude to write something. Compare with and without the Skill activated.

Step 2: Create a task-specific Skill.

Think of a task your team does repeatedly. Client proposal? Weekly report? Meeting summary? Create a Skill for it:
Skill Name: [Task Name] Generator

Instructions:
You are an expert at creating [task type] for [Company Name].

When the user provides [input type], you will:
1. [Step 1 of the process]
2. [Step 2]
3. [Step 3]

Output format: [describe the exact format]

Style: Follow our brand voice guide — [key points from your guide]

Always include: [required elements]
Never include: [things to avoid]

Step 3: Test Artifacts.

In your Project, try: "Create an interactive dashboard showing our team structure based on the team directory." Or: "Build a comparison table of our tools with costs, owners, and integration status." Watch Claude create something you can actually use and share.

Building one Skill is fun. Building a library of 20+ Skills that work together across departments — proposal Skills that reference pricing Skills that pull from your product catalog — that's implementation work. It requires understanding every team's workflows and designing Skills that compose well together.
