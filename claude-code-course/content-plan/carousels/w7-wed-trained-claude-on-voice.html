Step 1

I uploaded 14 newsletter issues + 20 LinkedIn posts to a folder.

Not my best writing. Not curated. Just a representative sample of how I actually write when I'm not overthinking it.

Newsletters, LinkedIn posts, a few client emails. Dropped them all in one folder on my computer.

Total time: 2 minutes.

Step 2

I asked Claude one question.

"Read everything in this folder. Describe my writing voice — tone, patterns, habits, what I avoid. Be specific."

That's it. One prompt. Claude read 34 pieces of content and started analyzing.

4 / 7
LOW / CODE
The Result

A 2-page voice profile more accurate than I'd write about myself.

  • My default sentence length (short, punchy, rarely over 15 words)
  • How I open posts (with a number or a surprising statement)
  • Words I repeat (deployed, compound, foundation, stack)
  • What I never do (no emojis, no exclamation marks, no "I'm excited to share")
  • My transition patterns (short fragments between sections)
  • How I close (call to action or a one-liner that reframes the whole piece)
