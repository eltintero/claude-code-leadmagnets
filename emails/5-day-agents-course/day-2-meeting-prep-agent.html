The Meeting Prep Agent

How many meetings do you walk into cold?

No context on the attendees. No recap of the last conversation. No idea what action items are still open.

You spend the first 5 minutes catching up. Or worse, you wing it and miss something important.

The Meeting Prep Agent fixes this. It runs 30-60 minutes before every calendar event and generates a briefing doc.
 

What the briefing includes

• Meeting title, time, and attendees

• Who each attendee is — role, last interaction, relevant context

• Agenda or likely topics

• Open action items from previous meetings

• Suggested talking points
Briefing: Q2 Review with Acme Corp
10:00 AM — 3 attendees

Sarah Chen, VP Operations
Last interaction: Mar 15 — discussed timeline delay on Phase 2.
She was frustrated about missed deadline.

Open items:
• Phase 2 revised timeline (you owe this)
• Contract renewal discussion (their contract expires Apr 30)

Suggested talking points:
• Lead with revised timeline to rebuild trust
• Transition to renewal — propose early renewal with scope expansion

You walk in prepared. Every time. Without doing the prep yourself.
 

The DIY version

Set up a Claude Cowork scheduled task that reads your calendar daily and drafts a briefing doc. Upload your client notes to a Project for context.

It's manual, but it gets you 60% of the way there.
The readiness check

This agent needs: calendar integration, client/contact notes in a structured knowledge base, and CRM data (optional but powerful).

Ask yourself: Is your client and contact information in a system Claude could access? Or is it spread across email threads, Slack DMs, and someone's notebook?

If it's scattered, the agent can only work with what you manually feed it. That's still useful — but limited.
The gap between DIY and professional

The DIY version works if your notes are organized and you remember to upload them. The professional version pulls data from your CRM, Slack, and email automatically — and generates briefings with deal history, sentiment analysis, and risk flags.

No manual uploads. No forgetting. Just a briefing waiting for you before every call.
