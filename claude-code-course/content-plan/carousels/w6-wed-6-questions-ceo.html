Questions 1 & 2

Q1: Who decides what AI can access?

Client data, pricing, internal docs — someone needs to own the boundaries. Not IT. Someone who understands the business.

Q2: How do you ensure consistent output across the team?

If 5 people use Claude with 5 different instructions, you get 5 different brands. Shared documentation solves this.

Questions 3 & 4

Q3: What's the review process before AI output reaches clients?

AI writes the first draft. A human reviews it. Every time. Without a clear process, someone will send an unreviewed AI email to your biggest client.

Q4: Who maintains the setup when things change?

Pricing changes. Team changes. Process changes. If nobody owns the documentation, your AI goes stale within weeks.

Question 5

What's the budget — and how do you measure ROI?

Claude Code costs ~$100/month per seat. Implementation costs time, training, and documentation effort.

The ROI metric isn't "hours saved." It's recovered capacity — what your team does with the time they get back.

A 10-person team saving 5 hrs/week each = $10K+/month in recovered capacity. The subscription is a rounding error.

Q6
Question 6

What happens when (not if) something goes wrong?

AI will hallucinate a number. Misquote a policy. Send a draft that needed editing. It will happen.

The question isn't "will it make mistakes?" The question is "do you have a review layer that catches them?"

Build the safety net before you need it.
