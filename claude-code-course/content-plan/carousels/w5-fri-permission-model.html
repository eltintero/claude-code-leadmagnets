The Analogy

Think of it like a new employee with supervised access.

You wouldn't give a new hire the keys to every system on day one. You'd give them access to what they need, watch how they use it, and expand from there.

Claude Code works the same way. You give it keys one at a time. You approve every action until you trust the pattern.

Permission Levels

3 levels. You choose.

Level 1
Read Only
Claude can read your files but can't change anything. Safest starting point.
Level 2
Create with Approval
Claude drafts changes. You review and approve each one before it happens.
Level 3
Autonomous
Claude executes approved categories of tasks on its own. You review outputs, not steps.

Boundaries

What Claude Code can't do.

  • Can't scan your entire computer — only folders you explicitly share
  • Can't access folders you haven't approved
  • Can't take actions without your permission (until you grant it)
  • Can't send emails, make purchases, or access the internet on its own
  • Can't remember between sessions unless you save context to files

You're always in control.

Privacy

Your conversations stay local.

Claude Code runs on your machine. Your files, your context, your business data — it stays on your computer.

Conversations aren't stored by Anthropic when you use the API. There's no training on your data.

This isn't ChatGPT where everything lives on someone else's server. Your data stays yours.
