Lesson 1

AI is 10% of the equation. Documentation is 90%.

The companies that fail with AI aren't bad at prompting. They're bad at documenting. They don't have their pricing written down. Their voice isn't defined. Their processes live in people's heads.

The tool is easy. The foundation is the work.

Lessons 2 & 3

Start with one department, not the whole org.

The companies that succeed pick sales, or ops, or content — prove it there, then expand. The ones that try to deploy company-wide on day one? Chaos.

Training without setup is useless. Setup without training is wasted.

You need both. A perfect CLAUDE.md with no one who knows how to use it is shelfware. A trained team with no documentation produces generic AI output.

Lesson 4

The ROI shows up in month 2, not week 1.

First month is calibration. You're building docs, training Claude, learning patterns. It feels slow. Some people give up here.

Month 2 is when it clicks. Claude knows your business. Your team knows the tool. Output quality jumps. Speed compounds.

The companies that quit in month 1 never experience what month 3 feels like.

Lesson 5

The businesses that go furthest have one internal champion.

Not the CEO. Not IT. One person who owns the AI setup, keeps the docs current, trains new hires, and pushes adoption.

Without a champion, AI adoption drifts. People revert to old habits. The docs go stale.

Find your champion. Give them the time. Everything else follows.
