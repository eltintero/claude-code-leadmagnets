## The DIY Path

Here's what it actually takes to build a complete Claude Code setup for a small business (5-15 people) on your own:

### Phase 1: Foundation (Week 1-2)
- Write CLAUDE.md files for your main work areas (sales, operations, marketing, client delivery)
- Document your brand voice, processes, and team structure
- Set up the desktop app for yourself and test it on real work
- Identify which integrations matter most for your business

### Phase 2: Integrations (Week 3-4)
- Connect your primary tools (Slack, email, calendar, Drive)
- Configure permissions for each tool
- Write integration-specific instructions in your CLAUDE.md
- Test each connection with real tasks
- Troubleshoot the things that don't work the first time (there will be several)

### Phase 3: Team Rollout (Week 5-8)
- Install and configure Claude Code for each team member
- Create role-specific CLAUDE.md files
- Set up shared skills and templates
- Define permission levels by role
- Train each person on how to use it effectively
- Create a feedback loop for corrections and improvements

### Phase 4: Optimization (Ongoing)
- Update CLAUDE.md files as your business evolves
- Add new skills for recurring workflows
- Adjust permissions based on what's working
- Monitor usage and ROI
- Maintain integrations as tools update

**Realistic timeline:** 2-3 months of part-time effort to get fully operational. Plus ongoing maintenance.

**Realistic cost:** Your subscription fees ($20-30/person/month) plus your time. For a 10-person team, figure 40-60 hours of setup and training time across those months. At whatever you value your time at, that's the real cost.

**What goes well:** You learn the tool deeply. You make decisions that fit your business exactly. You build internal knowledge about how AI works in your context.

**What goes wrong:** It takes longer than expected. The integrations are fiddly. Team adoption is uneven — some people love it, others resist. The CLAUDE.md files get outdated because nobody owns them. Three months in, half the team is using Claude effectively and the other half has gone back to doing things the old way.

This path works. Plenty of businesses have done it successfully. But it requires someone on your team who has the time, patience, and interest to own the project from start to finish.

## The Math

Let's put real numbers on this.

A 10-person team where each person spends 2 hours per day on tasks Claude could handle — emails, proposals, research, meeting prep, reporting, client follow-ups.

**2 hours/day x 10 people x 20 workdays/month = 400 hours/month** of potential AI-assisted work.

If Claude makes that work 50% faster (a conservative estimate for well-configured setups), you reclaim **200 hours per month**. At an average burdened cost of $50/hour, that's **$10,000/month in recovered capacity**.

The subscription cost for 10 people on the Team plan is $300/month.

The ROI math isn't subtle. But here's the catch: you only get that ROI if the setup actually works. If half the team isn't using it, or the CLAUDE.md files are stale, or the integrations aren't configured — you're paying for a tool that's running at 20% capacity.

The difference between a $300/month expense and a $10,000/month asset is entirely about implementation.
