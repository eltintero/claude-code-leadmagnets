The Daily Briefing Agent

Every Monday, someone on your team spends 2-4 hours compiling data from 5 different tools into a report.

Every morning, you spend 15-30 minutes triaging your inbox, checking dashboards, and figuring out what needs your attention today.

The Daily Briefing Agent runs at 8 AM and delivers a personalized summary. Everything you need to know. Nothing you don't.
 

What the briefing includes

• Today's calendar at a glance

• Key metrics snapshot (revenue, pipeline, support load)

• Items that need your attention — flagged by priority

• Relevant industry news or internal updates
Good morning. Here's your briefing for Tuesday, April 3.

4 meetings today — first at 9:30 AM (Q2 planning with marketing).

Needs attention:
• Deal with TechCo ($45K) has been stalled for 8 days — follow-up draft ready in your outbox.
• 2 support tickets escalated overnight (both billing-related).
• Sarah requested PTO approval — pending since Friday.

Metrics:
• Monthly revenue tracking 12% above target.
• Pipeline: 3 new opportunities added yesterday ($82K total).
• Support: avg response time up to 4.2 hrs (was 2.8 hrs last week).

No dashboard hopping. No inbox triage. You open one document and know exactly where to focus.
 

The DIY version

Set up a scheduled Cowork task that pulls your calendar and generates a morning summary. You can connect 1-2 data sources manually.

It won't have everything, but even a partial briefing saves 15 minutes a day.
The readiness check

This agent needs: connected tools (CRM, project management, support desk, calendar), defined KPIs you actually track, and data living in systems — not spreadsheets on someone's desktop.

Ask yourself: Are your key business metrics accessible through APIs, or do they require someone to manually pull numbers from 4 different tools?
The gap between DIY and professional

DIY: A decent summary from your calendar plus 1-2 tools. Better than nothing.

Professional: Connects your entire tech stack. Role-specific briefings for every person on the team. Sales lead gets pipeline updates. Ops lead gets project status. CEO gets the company-wide dashboard. All automated, customized, accurate.

One agent, many briefings, zero manual work.
