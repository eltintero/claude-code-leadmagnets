Your Team Now Has Something 90% of Companies Don't

Take a breath. Look at what you've built in four days:

  • Company Profile
  • Brand Voice Guide
  • Team Directory
  • Tool Stack Inventory
  • Process SOP (at least one)
  • FAQ Knowledge Base

That's a working AI knowledge base. Most companies that "deploy AI" skip all of this and wonder why it doesn't work.

But here's the thing: you wrote these docs from your perspective. You're one person. You have blind spots. Things you take for granted that a new employee (or AI) wouldn't know.

Today is about validation.

Today's Exercise • 30 minutes + async

Step 1: Share all documents with your team. Send them via Google Drive, Notion, or whatever your team uses. Include a clear ask: "Review these for accuracy. What's wrong? What's missing? What would confuse a new hire?"

Step 2: Set a 48-hour review deadline. Not a week. Two days. Urgency matters — this loses momentum fast if you let it drift.

Step 3: While you wait, test the knowledge base. Open your Claude Project and try these prompts:
"How do we onboard a new client?"

"Write an email to a prospect who asked about our pricing, in our brand voice."

"Who should I talk to about [specific tool] access?"

"What's our policy on [something from your FAQ]?"

Step 4: Note the quality gaps. Where does Claude give a great answer? Where does it get vague or wrong? The vague spots tell you exactly which docs need more detail.

Step 5: Consolidate feedback and update. Once reviews come back, update the docs and re-upload to your Project.
Quality standards for AI-ready docs:
• No assumed knowledge — spell out acronyms, explain context
• Specific, not vague — "send within 2 hours" not "follow up quickly"
• Decision points documented — "if X, do Y; if Z, do W"
• Updated quarterly (at minimum)
