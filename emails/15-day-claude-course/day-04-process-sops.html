Ask Claude "How Do We Onboard a Client?" and Watch It Nail the Answer

Here's the uncomfortable truth about most company documentation: it's written for humans who already have context.

"Follow up with the client after kickoff" — follow up how? Email? Call? What do you say? When? What if they don't respond?

AI needs zero assumed knowledge. Every step explicit. Every decision point documented. Every edge case addressed.

That sounds like a lot of work. It is — for the first one. But once you have the template, the rest flow much faster. And the payoff is massive: Claude can actually do the work, not just talk about it.

Today you'll create two document types: a Process SOP and an FAQ Knowledge Base.

Exercise 1: Process SOP • 20 minutes

Pick your most common, most repeated process. Client onboarding, proposal creation, new hire setup — whatever eats the most time. Open your Project and paste:
I want you to interview me to create a detailed Process SOP (Standard Operating Procedure) for: [YOUR PROCESS NAME]

Ask me one question at a time about:
- When this process starts (what triggers it)
- Every step in sequence
- Who is responsible for each step
- What tools are used at each step
- What the expected output of each step is
- Common mistakes or edge cases
- How long each step typically takes
- What "done" looks like

Important: Don't assume I've explained something. If a step seems vague, ask me to clarify. The goal is that someone with zero context could follow this document.

After the interview, generate a structured markdown document I can save as sop-[process-name].md

Exercise 2: FAQ Knowledge Base • 15 minutes
I want you to interview me to create an FAQ Knowledge Base for my company.

Ask me one at a time about:
- Questions new employees always ask in their first week
- Questions clients ask most frequently
- Questions that get asked repeatedly in Slack/email
- Common misconceptions about our products/services
- Policy questions (PTO, expenses, remote work, etc.)
- Technical questions that come up regularly

For each question, make sure I give you the COMPLETE answer, not just the short version.

After the interview, generate a structured markdown FAQ I can save as faq.md

Upload both to your Project. Then test it: ask Claude a question about the process you documented. Then ask about a process you haven't documented yet. The difference in answer quality will be obvious.
