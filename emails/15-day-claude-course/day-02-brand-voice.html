Every AI-Generated Email Sounds the Same. Fix It in 20 Minutes.

You can always tell when someone used AI to write an email. It's polished, professional, and completely devoid of personality.

That's because Claude defaults to a neutral, corporate tone. Technically correct. Utterly forgettable.

Your Brand Voice Guide is the single highest-leverage document you can create for AI. Why? Because it affects every single output — every email, every proposal, every social post, every client response. One document, multiplied across thousands of interactions.

A good voice guide covers:

  • Overall tone (formal? casual? somewhere specific in between?)
  • Vocabulary — words you use, words you never use
  • Formality level by context (client email vs. internal Slack)
  • Real examples of writing that nails your voice
  • Anti-examples of writing that misses

Later this week, you'll turn this into a Claude Skill — a reusable instruction set that any team member can activate with one click.

Today's Exercise • 20 minutes

Step 1: Open your AI Knowledge Base Project from yesterday and start a new conversation. Paste this prompt:
I want you to interview me to create a Brand Voice Guide for my company.

Ask me one question at a time about:
- Our overall tone (formal/casual/somewhere in between)
- How we want to come across to clients
- Words and phrases we use regularly
- Words and phrases we NEVER use
- Formality level
- How our voice differs by context

Then ask me to paste 2-3 examples of writing I'm proud of and 1-2 examples I don't want.

After the interview, generate a structured markdown file I can save as brand-voice.md

Step 2: Have the conversation. The examples are the most important part — Claude learns more from 3 real writing samples than from 20 adjectives describing your tone.

Step 3: Save as brand-voice.md and upload to your Project.

Bonus: Turn it into a Claude Skill. Go to your Profile → Skills → Create Skill. Name it "Write in [Company] Voice" and paste the guide as the instruction. Now any team member can activate your voice with one click.

When we do this for clients, we analyze actual communication patterns — emails, proposals, social posts — and extract nuances most people can't articulate about their own voice. It's one of the highest-ROI pieces of our discovery work.
