Before Your Team Uses Claude, Do This First

79% of AI deployments fail.

Not because the technology doesn't work. Not because teams don't try. Because nobody teaches the AI about the business first.

Think about it: when you hire a new employee, you don't sit them at a desk and say "figure it out." You onboard them. You explain the company, the culture, the processes, the clients.

Claude is no different.

Without context, Claude gives you generic, technically-correct-but-useless answers. With context, it gives you answers that sound like they came from someone who's worked at your company for years.
Here's what separates companies where AI sticks from the 79% where it fails: documentation. Not AI documentation — YOUR business documentation. Packaged so Claude can actually use it.

Claude Projects are persistent knowledge hubs. You upload documents, set custom instructions, and every conversation in that Project has full context about your business.

Today, you're going to create your master AI Project and start with the most important document: your Company Profile.

The trick? Use Claude itself to interview you and generate the document. It's faster, more thorough, and it forces you to articulate things you've never written down.

Today's Exercise • 25 minutes

Step 1: Create a new Claude Project called "[Your Company Name] — AI Knowledge Base"

Step 2: Set these custom instructions: "You are an AI assistant for [Company Name]. Always reference the uploaded knowledge base documents when answering questions. Maintain our brand voice. If you don't have enough information to answer accurately, say so."

Step 3: Start a conversation in the Project and paste this prompt:
I want you to interview me to create a Company Profile document for my business.

Ask me questions one at a time about:
- What our company does (products/services)
- Who our ideal customers are (and who we DON'T serve)
- Our business model (revenue streams, pricing)
- How many employees/contractors we have
- Our org structure
- Our mission and what makes us different
- Our growth trajectory and goals

After the interview, generate a structured markdown document I can save as company-profile.md

Step 4: Answer Claude's questions honestly and completely. The more detail you give, the better every future AI interaction will be.

Step 5: Save the generated document and upload it to your Project. Share with leadership for a quick review — they'll catch things you missed.

Full disclosure: this is a simplified version of what we do in our discovery audits. We interview every department head, map every process, and build the complete AI knowledge base in 2-3 weeks. But you can absolutely start this yourself — and that's exactly what this course is for.
