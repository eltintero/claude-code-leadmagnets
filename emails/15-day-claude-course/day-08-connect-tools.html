Claude Now Knows What Your Team Said in Slack Yesterday

Your knowledge base documents are static — they capture how things work. But your business is dynamic. Conversations happen in Slack. Deals move through your CRM. Documents get updated in Drive. Tasks shift in Asana.

Claude Connectors bridge this gap. They give Claude access to your live business data.

Available connectors include: Gmail, Google Drive, Slack, Notion, Asana, Linear, Jira, Stripe, Salesforce, HubSpot, Zendesk, and more. New ones ship regularly.

The technical term is MCP — Model Context Protocol. Think of it as "USB-C for AI." A standard way to plug any tool into Claude.
Enterprise Search is where it gets powerful. Once tools are connected, Claude can search across ALL of them simultaneously. "What did the team decide about the Q4 budget?" pulls from Slack conversations, Google Docs, and email threads — all at once.

Combined with your knowledge base, Claude now has two layers: documented knowledge (how things work) and live data (what's happening now). That's when it starts feeling like a real team member.

Today's Exercise • 20 minutes

Step 1: Connect 2-3 tools.

Go to Claude Settings → Connectors. Start with the tools your team uses most. Google Drive and Slack are usually the highest-value starting points. Follow the OAuth flow for each.

Step 2: Test real queries.

In your Project, try questions that span tools:
"Search Slack for any discussion about [recent project] in the last 2 weeks."

"Find the latest version of [document name] in our Google Drive."

"What tasks are assigned to [person] in [project management tool] right now?"

"Summarize the email thread with [client name] from last week."

Step 3: Explore Enterprise Search.

Try the "Ask Your Org" sidebar feature. This searches across all connected tools simultaneously. Ask a question about a recent decision or project status.

Connecting 2-3 tools is straightforward. Designing an org-wide connector architecture — with proper security scoping, data governance, access controls across 20-50 tools and multiple departments — that's where it gets complex. Who gets access to what? How do you prevent sensitive data from leaking across teams? How do you audit usage?
