A 20-Minute Research Report Better Than Your Analyst's

Regular Claude conversations are reactive: you ask, it answers. Research mode is different. It's agentic — Claude plans an approach, executes multiple searches, follows leads, synthesizes findings, and delivers a structured report with citations.

A research task might take 5-45 minutes. During that time, Claude is doing the work a research analyst would: identifying sources, cross-referencing information, building a coherent narrative from scattered data points.

The output includes citations, so you can verify anything. This isn't "trust me" — it's "here's what I found and where I found it."

Where it gets really interesting: combine Research mode with your connected tools. Claude can research external information AND cross-reference it with your internal data. "How does our pricing compare to competitors?" pulls from public competitor data AND your internal pricing docs.

Today's Exercise • 30 minutes

Step 1: Run a real research task.

Pick something your team actually needs. Ideas:

  • "Research [competitor name]. What are they doing well? Where are they weak? How does their pricing and positioning compare to ours?"
  • "Research the latest trends in [your industry] for 2025-2026. What should we be paying attention to?"
  • "Research best practices for [specific challenge your team faces]. Give me actionable recommendations."

Step 2: Review the output. Check the citations. Notice the structure. Compare it to how long this would have taken manually.

Step 3: Write down 3 questions you wish you could ask AI about your own business.

Questions that require both external research and internal data. "How does our client retention compare to industry benchmarks?" "Which of our services has the best margin relative to competitors?" "What skills gaps do we have compared to where the industry is heading?"
Those 3 questions you just wrote down? They're the seed of your AI roadmap. The questions you can't fully answer yet — because the data isn't connected or documented — those are the highest-value problems to solve next.
