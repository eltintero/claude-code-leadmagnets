The Company Knowledge Agent

Every company has one person who knows everything.

How to onboard a client. Where the SOPs live. Who handles billing for enterprise accounts. What the refund policy actually says.

That person spends 30-60 minutes every day answering the same 20 questions. They're a bottleneck, and they know it. But nobody's fixed it because the knowledge lives in their head.

The Company Knowledge Agent fixes this.

It's an AI that knows your business — products, processes, policies, people, terminology — and answers questions instantly, accurately, in your voice.
 

What it looks like in practice

New hire asks: "What's our refund policy?"
→ Instant, correct answer with the right SOP linked.

Sales rep asks: "Who handles billing for enterprise accounts?"
→ Name, role, contact info. No Slack thread needed.

Manager asks: "What's the process for requesting a new tool?"
→ Step-by-step from your ops playbook.

The result: Your go-to person gets 4+ hours back per week. New hires become self-sufficient on Day 1 instead of Week 3.
 

The DIY version

Create a Claude Project, upload your company docs — SOPs, FAQ, team directory — and set custom instructions. Your team can chat with it in claude.ai.

It works. And it's a great first step.
The readiness check

This agent requires your company knowledge to be documented — company profile, SOPs, FAQ, team directory, policies.

Ask yourself: Could a new hire find the answer to "How do we onboard a client?" without asking anyone?

If the answer is no, your knowledge lives in people's heads, not in a system. That's the #1 thing to fix before deploying any AI.
The honest truth

This is a beginner version. A Claude Project with a few docs answers questions — but it can still hallucinate, it can't connect to your live tools, and it doesn't proactively surface information.

A properly built AI employee is trained on your complete knowledge base (not just a few docs), connected to Slack and your CRM, follows your brand voice perfectly, and has guardrails that prevent wrong answers.

That's what we build at LowCode — and the difference is night and day.
