The Support Draft Agent

Every support ticket follows a pattern. Agent reads the ticket. Looks up the answer. Types a response. Sends it. 5-10 minutes per ticket.

Most of those tickets are variations of questions you've answered hundreds of times.

The Support Draft Agent pre-drafts responses from your playbook. Before your team even opens the ticket, a draft is waiting.
 

What it does

• Reads incoming tickets automatically

• Matches the question to your knowledge base

• Drafts a response in your brand voice

• Flags it for human review before sending
Ticket: "How do I update my billing info?"

Draft response (ready before agent opens ticket):

"Hi! You can update your billing info in Settings → Billing → Payment Method. Click 'Edit' next to your current card, enter the new details, and hit Save. Changes apply to your next billing cycle.

If you need to update the billing contact or company name, just reply here and I'll take care of it."

Agent action: Review. Click send. 30 seconds.

The DIY version

Create a Claude Project with your FAQ and support playbook. When a ticket comes in, paste it into the Project and ask for a draft response. Copy the draft back to your support tool.

Manual, but it cuts response time in half even with the copy-paste overhead.
The readiness check

This agent needs: a support playbook, comprehensive FAQ, brand voice guide, and access to your support tool.

The quality of the drafts is directly proportional to the quality of your documentation. Garbage in, garbage out.
The gap between DIY and professional

DIY: Manual copy-paste. Works for low volume. Falls apart at scale.

Professional: Direct integration with your support tool. Auto-drafts appear in the agent's queue. Smart escalation rules route complex tickets to senior reps. The agent gets smarter over time as it learns from corrections.

The Pattern

Look at all 5 agents we covered this week. Every single one requires the same foundation:

• Documented processes — SOPs, playbooks, workflows

• Structured knowledge — FAQ, company info, product details

• Connected tools — CRM, calendar, support desk, project management

• Clear guidelines — brand voice, escalation rules, quality standards

Without this foundation, AI agents hallucinate, give wrong answers, and create more work than they save.

This is why 79% of AI implementations fail. Not because the AI is bad. Because the foundation isn't there.
